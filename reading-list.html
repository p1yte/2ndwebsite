Books

Here are some of the books I’ve read and recommend, covering various topics like studying, self-improvement, novels, and more.

Stoner

Author: John Williams

Stoner by John Williams was one of the first novels I ever read, and it is still my favorite. The writing is absolutely beautiful, drawing me into a peaceful, almost familiar setting. I was captivated by the complex character dynamics, especially the exploration of relationship abuse through the stoic lens of Stoner. His quiet discovery of love, both for literature and in his personal relationships, was deeply inspiring to me. The tender relationship with his daughter moved me, and this book played a huge role in making me fall in love with fiction.

Can't Hurt Me

Author: David Goggins

I went into Can't Hurt Me thinking David Goggins was invincible, capable of achieving anything through sheer willpower. But what struck me most was realizing how human he is—once overweight, lost, and struggling. His journey to mastering his mind and developing "mental calluses" was truly inspiring. I was surprised to learn it took him multiple tries to break the pull-up record and complete SEAL training, proving persistence is everything. Goggins' constant question, “Can I take one more step?” sticks with me because the answer is always yes.

A Mind for Numbers

Author: Barbara Oakley

I really enjoyed A Mind for Numbers by Barbara Oakley. Growing up with little confidence in math, this book opened my eyes to different thinking strategies—focused and diffused modes—and the importance of the German concept of Einstellung (insight-based problem solving). The clear examples made complex ideas easy to understand. I think it’s a must-read for any student studying math or science, as it not only boosts comprehension but also builds confidence in learning these subjects.

Project Hail Mary

Author: Andy Weir

Project Hail Mary was my first science fiction book, and I was completely surprised by how immersed I became in the story. Andy Weir did an incredible job making it feel like a movie I was watching. Every time I stopped reading, I couldn't wait to start again. The characters were fantastic, and the entire book was a rollercoaster ride. The story made me realize how much I still have to learn about the natural world—especially in physics and chemistry. It also deepened my appreciation for mathematics as the language of our universe and left me with a sense of moral responsibility to better understand it.

Deep Work

Author: Cal Newport

Deep Work is an incredibly relevant book in today’s ultra-digital world. Thanks to this book and Newport’s other work, Digital Minimalism, I haven’t had social media on my phone for over three years and have drastically reduced my phone use. Newport's focus on intentional living and regaining control of our time truly changed my approach to work. Many, including myself, have wasted hours in the library—distracted by phones and websites, which often serve as mental pacifiers when we are uncomfortable. Newport’s philosophy transformed how I work, helping me focus deeply and become more productive. I highly recommend it to all students and knowledge workers.

East of Eden

Author: John Steinback

I was captivated by East of Eden, particularly the complex relationships between Charles and Adam Trask and Cal and Aron. Steinbeck's decision to link their struggles to the biblical story of Cain and Abel added so much depth, illustrating the timeless conflict between good and evil. Especially Cal's realization that he can reject the darker sides of himself, was incredibly thought-provoking. However, it’s not just about rejection—Cal learns to observe and understand these darker impulses, realizing they don’t define him. The generational patterns, the weight of inherited guilt, and the longing for acceptance all made this novel a rich exploration of human nature. East of Eden wasn’t just a story; it felt like a meditation on family, identity, and the eternal struggle between light and darkness within us all.

Youtube

Channles that I think bring a lot of value.

3Blue1Brown

3Blue1Brown makes awesome videos on math, physics, and computer science with super cool animations. Their visuals really help make tricky topics easier to understand.

The Organic Chemistry Tutor

The Organic Chemistry Tutor really helps me with my math classes, especially when it came to understanding calculus. The explanations are super clear and detailed, making tough concepts easier to grasp. It’s been a lifesaver for reviewing material and preparing for exams!

jvscholz

jvscholz makes awesome tech videos, especially focusing on e-ink devices and older technology. He’s super disciplined, streaming his study sessions for over 8 hours a day for more than a year! His content really got me into e-ink devices and shows how dedicated he is to learning. I love his channel because it combines tech with serious student motivation, and his productivity inspires me every day.

Alex Hormozi

Alex is the best source of business advice I’ve found. He manages to motivate and deliver tons of practical information at the same time. His videos are clear, direct, and packed with strategies for scaling businesses, improving marketing, and developing a strong mindset. It feels like a personal mentorship, and the value he provides for free is unmatched. His content is a must-watch for anyone looking to level up their business.

Podcasts

I find these guys very interesting and I learn something new every episode.

Lex Fridman

I love Lex Fridman’s podcast because he invites people from all backgrounds and engages them in open, thoughtful conversations. He debates ideas respectfully and truly believes in learning from everyone, regardless of their beliefs. He's also incredibly smart, and I always learn a lot from his episodes.

Andrew Huberman

I really admire Andrew Huberman because, despite his work at Stanford, he offers all of his podcast content for free to the public. He provides incredible advice on physical and mental health, with deep scientific insights. While the information can be a lot to absorb, I always walk away with actionable tips that improve my daily life. His generosity and dedication to sharing knowledge with everyone are truly inspiring!

Chris Williamson

I really like Chris Williamson's podcast because it feels like a conversation with a friend, not an overload of information. But don’t get me wrong—his episodes are still packed with valuable insights. It’s entertaining and informative, making it my absolute favorite podcast.
